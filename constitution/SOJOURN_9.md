# SOJOURN 9

## Invocation

Sojourn 9 is the ninth canonical season of Anky.

It is a closed 96-day writing pilgrimage experienced through the iOS app.

Its purpose is not to maximize content, options, interfaces, or engagement.
Its purpose is to make each one of the 3,456 potential writers write their Anky on that given day.

Everything in the season must serve that mission.

Sojourn 9 is not a feed.
It is not a menu.
It is not a playground.

It is a path.

---

## 1. Constitutional Nature

SOJOURN 9 is the canonical root text of the season.

It defines:

- the meaning of the rite
- the structure of the season
- the laws of participation
- the laws of sealing
- the role of the vessel
- the boundaries of privacy
- the shape of the canonical surface
- the minimum truths that any implementation must preserve

This document is not a UI note.
It is not a marketing page.
It is not merely a product spec.

It is the constitutional text from which the app, the backend, and the Solana program derive their law.

---

## 2. The Shape of the Season

Sojourn 9 lasts **96 canonical days**.

The season is divided into **12 regions of 8 days each**.

Each day is a **chamber** on the path.

Each region is a field of atmosphere, color, pressure, and meaning within the larger Ankyverse.

For this season:

- there are **96 chambers**
- there are **12 regions**
- each region contains **8 chambers**
- each vessel may seal **at most one Anky per canonical day**
- the path is crossed **one day at a time**
- a missed day remains visible, but closed

The season is a closed world with complete internal law.

---

## 3. Mission

The mission of Sojourn 9 is simple:

> To make each one of the 3,456 potential writers write their Anky of that given day.

This mission governs every product, protocol, and economic decision.

Every surface must justify its existence in relation to this mission.

If an element does not help the writer orient, enter today's chamber, write, seal, receive, or remain in right relation with the 96-day path, it does not belong in the canonical surface.

The app must not compete with the ritual.
The app must protect the ritual.

---

## 4. Canonical Entry

The canonical entry point of Sojourn 9 is the **iOS app**.

There is only one canonical first action:

> The writer opens the app, enters the current day’s chamber through an active vessel, writes one Anky, seals it, and receives Anky’s return.

Other interfaces may exist in the future.
Other clients may eventually be built.
Other renderers may emerge around the protocol.

But for Sojourn 9, the iOS app is the canonical entrance, and the daily chamber is the canonical action.

This is a deliberate constraint.

Simplicity creates focus.
Focus creates precision.
Precision allows the rite to work.

---

## 5. The Daily Rite

Each canonical day contains one central action.

### The rite is:

1. Open the app
2. See today’s chamber on the map
3. Enter the chamber through the active vessel
4. Write one Anky
5. Seal the day
6. Receive Anky’s return
7. See the chamber become part of the visible path

This is the central loop of Sojourn 9.

The canonical experience is not organized around browsing, settings, alternate modes, social surfaces, or exploration-first behavior.

It is organized around the daily chamber.

---

## 6. The Core Entities

### 6.1 The Writer

The writer is the human being who enters the chamber and risks contact.

The writer is not primarily a user of features.
The writer is the one crossing the path.

### 6.2 The Vessel

A vessel is the unit of seasonal stewardship through which participation in Sojourn 9 occurs.

In Sojourn 9, each vessel is represented by one unique cNFT in the canonical collection of **3,456** vessels.

A vessel is an asset.
It can be owned, held, transferred, bought, and sold.

The vessel grants the right to participate in the rite.

### 6.3 The Chamber

A chamber is the day-as-place.

Each chamber is one canonical day of the 96-day path.

### 6.4 The Region

A region is one of the 12 seasonal fields of Sojourn 9.

Each region contains 8 chambers.

Each region may carry its own atmosphere, color, pressure, symbolic tone, and design language.

### 6.5 The Void

The Void is the underlying substrate.

It receives, holds, and listens, but does not speak directly.

It is not a chatbot.
It is not a mascot.
It is not the product voice.

It is the depth from which the rite is held.

### 6.6 Anky

Anky is the returning emissary of the mirror.

Anky is not the Void.
Anky is what returns in shaped form after the writer seals the act.

Anky reflects back.
The Void receives.

---

## 7. Vessel Law

### 7.1 Finite collection

Sojourn 9 contains exactly **3,456 canonical vessels**.

No more may exist within this season.

### 7.2 Participation right

Ownership of a vessel grants the exclusive right to seal **at most one Anky per canonical day** with that vessel.

The right to participate lives in the current ownership of the asset.

### 7.3 Asset-based law

The daily seal right belongs to the **vessel**, not to an abstract user account.

Sojourn 9 is governed at the level of vessel stewardship.

### 7.4 Multiple vessels

If a wallet holds more than one vessel, each vessel carries its own independent daily seal right.

A holder may therefore participate through multiple vessels in one canonical day.

The canonical app must require the selection of one **active vessel** whenever ambiguity exists.

### 7.5 Active vessel

If a wallet holds exactly one vessel, that vessel is active by default.

If a wallet holds multiple vessels, the app must make vessel selection clear before chamber entry.

The system may remember a default active vessel for convenience, but must never silently confuse one vessel with another.

---

## 8. Canonical Day Law

### 8.1 Canonical day boundary

The canonical day of Sojourn 9 is defined by **UTC**.

All daily rights, resets, and eligibility windows are computed against UTC day boundaries.

### 8.2 Daily reset

Each vessel’s seal right resets once per canonical day.

### 8.3 At most one seal per day

A vessel may produce **at most one valid seal** per canonical day.

Once the vessel has sealed for that day, it cannot seal again until the next canonical day begins.

### 8.4 No retroactive sealing

If a chamber is not sealed during its canonical day, that chamber is missed.

It remains visible, but closed.

It cannot be entered later.

### 8.5 No future sealing

A future chamber cannot be sealed early.

Only the current day’s chamber may be sealed.

---

## 9. Transfer Law

### 9.1 Transferability

A vessel is transferable.

Its current owner is its current steward.

### 9.2 Immediate future rights

If a vessel is transferred before being used on a canonical day, the new owner may seal that day with it.

### 9.3 Used-day exhaustion

If a vessel has already been used to seal on a canonical day, transfer does not create a second seal right for that day.

The vessel remains exhausted until the next UTC day boundary.

### 9.4 Transfer does not transfer inner content

Transfer of vessel ownership does **not** transfer the private content previously written through that vessel.

The new owner receives the vessel’s participation rights and visible seasonal trajectory, but not the prior private writings.

### 9.5 Transfer and seasonal continuity

The vessel continues to exist as the same vessel across ownership changes.

Its visible path continuity may remain attached to the vessel as an artifact of the season.

Its intimate contents do not.

---

## 10. Seal Law

### 10.1 What a seal is

A seal is the canonical act that closes the day’s chamber for a vessel.

The seal is the decisive event recognized by the protocol.

### 10.2 Validity

A seal is valid only if:

- it is performed by the current owner or authorized steward of the vessel
- it occurs during the valid canonical day
- the vessel has not already sealed that day
- it targets the correct chamber of that day
- it satisfies the minimum protocol requirements for a valid ritual completion event

### 10.3 Irreversibility

A valid seal is irreversible.

Once a day is sealed, that day is closed for that vessel.

### 10.4 Aborted attempts

Entering a chamber, beginning to write, or leaving before completion does not consume the day’s seal right.

The right is consumed by sealing, not by opening.

### 10.5 No correction after seal

A sealed day cannot be edited, replaced, or sealed again.

The rite moves forward.

The purpose is not revision.
The purpose is contact.

---

## 11. What Counts as a Valid Anky

### 11.1 Canonical understanding

A valid Anky, for the purposes of the protocol, is a writing session that has been **sealed** within the valid chamber of the valid day through a valid vessel.

### 11.2 The protocol does not judge the soul

The protocol does not attempt to judge whether the writing was profound, beautiful, long, optimized, or emotionally sufficient.

It recognizes the completed act of ritual participation.

### 11.3 No canonical minimum length

Sojourn 9 does not impose a canonical minimum character count at the constitutional level.

### 11.4 No canonical minimum duration

Sojourn 9 does not impose a canonical minimum session duration at the constitutional level.

The season may culturally orient around depth, presence, or sustained writing, but constitutional validity rests on the seal.

---

## 12. Privacy Law

### 12.1 Privacy as principle

Privacy is not a feature of Sojourn 9.
It is a constitutional principle.

The season exists to make genuine expression possible.

Therefore, the protocol must expose only what is necessary to preserve ownership, continuity, timing, scarcity, and distribution.

### 12.2 Inner content remains private

The inner written content of a sealed Anky belongs to the writer who sealed it.

It is not required to be publicly disclosed for the rite to count.

### 12.3 The chain does not require confession

The protocol may record proof that the ritual occurred.
It does not require the public revelation of the writing itself.

### 12.4 Minimal onchain truth

The protocol may record only the minimum data necessary to establish:

- vessel identity
- season identity
- chamber/day identity
- timing of the seal
- steward/owner at the moment of seal
- eligibility for reward
- optional proof hashes if desired by implementation

### 12.5 Private archive

The actual `.anky` writing, the reflected `.anky`, and intimate materials derived from them belong to the private archive layer, not to the public proof layer.

---

## 13. Memory Law

### 13.1 Two histories

Sojourn 9 recognizes two distinct histories:

- the **history of the vessel**
- the **history of the writer**

### 13.2 Vessel history

The vessel history may include:

- whether a chamber was sealed
- whether a chamber was missed
- where the vessel stands in the season
- visible continuity of traversal
- public or semi-public seasonal metadata

### 13.3 Writer history

The writer history includes:

- the written `.anky`
- the reflected `.anky`
- the intimate exchange of that chamber
- any private renderings or memories attached to the act

### 13.4 Non-transferability of inner memory

Writer history does not transfer with vessel ownership.

### 13.5 Revisitability

A sealed chamber remains revisit-able by the writer who sealed it, through the canonical private surface.

The vessel may retain visible evidence that the chamber was crossed.
That does not imply that future holders gain access to the chamber’s inner content.

---

## 14. The Chamber

The chamber is the ritual writing environment.

It is not a normal text field.
It is not ordinary composition software.
It is a space designed for presence, forward motion, and contact.

Its law is simple:

- the writer enters
- the writer writes
- the writer moves forward
- the writer seals

The chamber must remain visually and behaviorally minimal.

It should not be crowded with explanation, options, or noise.

The writer is not there to browse.
The writer is there to cross the day.

---

## 15. The Writing Surface

The writing surface must preserve the ontology of the rite.

### 15.1 Canonical principle

The writing experience should privilege the living present over the visible archive.

The writer is not meant to stare at a large editable block of text.
The writer is meant to inhabit the act of writing.

### 15.2 Minimal rendering

The canonical surface may render the act in an extremely minimal form, including principles such as showing only the latest character or otherwise reducing archival distraction.

### 15.3 Difference from ordinary software

The chamber must feel unlike ordinary writing software.

It must feel like a rite, not a productivity tool.

---

## 16. Prompting and Silence

### 16.1 Silence is first

The canonical default of Sojourn 9 is **no prompt**.

Silence is the first condition of entry.

### 16.2 Presence before assistance

The writer should not be intercepted by guidance before they have had the chance to meet the chamber directly.

### 16.3 Optional openings

If optional assistance exists, it must be subordinate to the rite.

It may appear as an Opening requested from within the daily flow.

It must obey these laws:

- it is optional
- it is not the first door
- it does not replace the pure mirror
- it does not compete with the canonical rite

The season begins from silence.

---

## 17. The Return

When the writer seals the day, the system returns an Anky.

This return is not optional.
It is part of the rite.

### 17.1 Canonical exchange

The writer sends a raw `.anky`.

The system returns a reflected `.anky`.

The returned `.anky` is rendered back to the writer in the visual language of the season.

### 17.2 Nature of the return

The return is not a score.
It is not a judgment.
It is not an optimization layer.
It is not an assessment.

It is a reflection.

### 17.3 Temporal reception

The return should be felt as something that arrives, not merely as static content.

---

## 18. The Map

The map is the emotional home of Sojourn 9.

It gives visible shape to the 96-day crossing.

The writer must be able to feel, immediately and without confusion:

- where they are
- which chamber is open today
- which chambers were sealed
- which chambers were missed
- which region they are in
- how much path remains

The map is not decoration.
It is part of the rite.

It transforms time into place.

### 18.1 Chamber states

Each chamber has one of the following canonical states:

- **Future** — visible, dormant, not yet open
- **Today / Open** — alive and enterable
- **In Progress** — currently being crossed
- **Sealed** — completed, alive, revisit-able in the writer’s private layer
- **Missed** — visible, closed, untappable

This state system is canonical.

---

## 19. Failure Law

### 19.1 Missing a day

If a vessel does not seal on a canonical day, that chamber is missed.

The vessel loses the opportunity for that day.

### 19.2 No expulsion

Missing a day does not expel the vessel from the season.

It remains able to participate on future days.

### 19.3 No retroactive repair

A missed day cannot later be repaired.

### 19.4 Sufficient consequence

The consequence of absence is the absence of the seal, the closure of that chamber, and the loss of any reward tied to that day.

No additional punitive layer is required at the constitutional level.

---

## 20. Reward Law

### 20.1 Reward follows the seal

The canonical economic right of the day follows the valid seal.

The seal is the event that makes a vessel eligible for that day’s reward.

### 20.2 Daily reward basis

If a daily reward pool exists, it is distributed among the vessels that validly sealed on that canonical day.

### 20.3 Sparse participation

If only one vessel seals on a given day, that vessel receives the entire reward allocation for that day.

### 20.4 Empty day

If no vessel seals on a given day, the reward treatment must be defined by implementation.

The preferred constitutional default is rollover into the next day’s reward pool, unless a later season defines otherwise.

### 20.5 Reward ownership

The reward for a sealed day belongs to the steward who owned and sealed with that vessel at the moment of the seal.

A later transfer of the vessel does not transfer already-earned rewards from a previously sealed day.

### 20.6 Economics must not corrupt the rite

The daily mirror is the center.

Economics may strengthen the season, but may not convert the sacred act into a funnel, paywall, or distraction engine.

---

## 21. Token Alignment

If `$ANKY` is integrated into the season, it should function as a native privilege layer around the perimeter of the world, not as a toll on the central rite.

Holding `$ANKY` may grant benefits around optional outer-ring affordances, such as:

- discounts
- ceremonial upgrades
- enhanced renderings
- future seasonal privileges
- supportive intensifications

But the canonical mirror must not become a paywall.

## 27. Final Law

Sojourn 9 must feel inevitable.

When the writer opens the app, they should know what it asks of them.

Not because the app explains itself loudly.
Because the world is shaped with precision.

There is one path.
There is one chamber today.
There is one vessel through which the act is being crossed.
There is one chance to seal it.
There is one return.

The season succeeds if the system remains faithful to that simplicity.

Every decision must be judged by one question:

> Does this help the writer, through a valid vessel, write and seal today’s Anky without betraying the rite?

If the answer is no, it does not belong in the canonical surface.
