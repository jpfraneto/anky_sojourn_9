# SOJOURN 9

## Invocation

Sojourn 9 is the ninth canonical season of Anky.

It is a finite daily rite of return.
It is a 96-day writing pilgrimage divided into 12 regions of 8 days.
It is entered through the iOS app.
It is bounded by exactly 3,456 vessels.

Each vessel is a soulbound cNFT.
One wallet may steward at most one vessel.

The center is daily writing, not trading, spectacle, or social performance.

---

## 1. Constitutional Nature

This text is the law of Sojourn 9.

The app, backend, and Solana program derive from it.
If implementation conflicts with this text, this text wins.

---

## 2. Mission

The mission of Sojourn 9 is simple:

> Make each one of the 3,456 potential writers write and seal their Anky of that day.

Sojourn 9 is designed as continuity, not episodic engagement.
Each day is a crossing, not a content event.

---

## 3. Canonical Human Act

The canonical human act of Sojourn 9 is:

1. claim vessel
2. write one raw `.anky`
3. compute the hash on device
4. seal the commitment onchain
5. continue the crossing

This act takes place through the canonical iOS app.

---

## 4. Canonical Entry

The canonical entrance is the iOS app.

While supply remains, the app invites an unclaimed wallet to claim its vessel.
Claim is deliberate.

After claim, today’s chamber is the first recurring action.

---

## 5. Season Shape

Sojourn 9 contains:

- 96 canonical days
- 12 regions
- 8 chambers per region
- one chamber per day

The canonical day is UTC.

Only the current chamber may be sealed.
A missed chamber remains visible, but closed.
There is no retroactive sealing and no future sealing.

---

## 6. Vessel Law

Sojourn 9 contains exactly 3,456 canonical vessels.

Each valid vessel is a cNFT in the canonical Sojourn 9 collection.
Each valid vessel is soulbound for the duration of the season.

A wallet may steward at most one valid vessel.
Once claimed, the vessel remains bound to its claiming wallet for the season.

Each vessel may seal at most one Anky per UTC day.

The season does not rely on transfer-time reassignment of daily rights or reward rights.
The canonical surface must not introduce selected-vessel ambiguity.

The intended shape of the season is simple:
one writer, one vessel, one daily crossing.

---

## 7. Seal Law

A seal closes the current chamber for a valid vessel.

The seal is a commitment, not a publication.
The chain records commitment, not content.

The proof exists to protect the rite, not to replace it.

A valid seal requires:

- the current steward of a valid vessel
- the current canonical day
- the correct chamber of that day
- no prior seal for that vessel on that UTC day
- the device-computed hash commitment of the exact raw `.anky` string

A valid seal is irreversible.
The day’s right is consumed by sealing.
A sealed day cannot be edited, replaced, or sealed again.

---

## 8. Privacy Law

Privacy is constitutional, not incidental.

The rite is private.
The proof is public.

The chain exists to verify commitment and stewardship, not to expose writing.

The raw `.anky`, the reflected `.anky`, and the writer’s private archive remain offchain.

The public layer may record only the minimum truth required for:

- vessel identity
- season identity
- chamber/day identity
- timing of the seal
- steward at the moment of seal
- reward eligibility
- the device-computed `.anky` hash commitment

---

## 9. Memory Law

Sojourn 9 recognizes two histories:

- the history of the vessel
- the history of the writer

Vessel history may show sealed chambers, missed chambers, and visible continuity of traversal.

Writer history includes the raw `.anky`, the reflected `.anky`, and the private exchange of the chamber.

Vessel history does not grant access to the writer’s inner archive.

---

## 10. Chamber, Return, and Map

The chamber is the ritual writing environment.

It is not a general journaling app.
It is not ordinary composition software.
It is a minimal space for presence, forward motion, and contact.

Silence is first.
Optional openings, if they exist, must remain subordinate to the rite.

When the writer seals the day, Anky returns in reflected `.anky` form.
This return is part of the rite.

The map gives visible shape to the crossing.
Its canonical chamber states are:

- Future
- Today / Open
- In Progress
- Sealed
- Missed

---

## 11. Failure and Reward

If a vessel does not seal on a canonical day, that chamber is missed.
The season does not expel the vessel for missing a day.
A missed day cannot be repaired later.

If a daily reward pool exists, reward follows the valid seal.
If no vessel seals on a given day, the preferred constitutional default is rollover.

Economics may support the season, but may not corrupt the rite.

---

## 12. Canonical Surface

The canonical surface is simple.

Its center is today’s chamber.

The canonical surfaces are:

1. Sojourn Map
2. Writing Chamber
3. Return Chamber
4. Sealed Chamber View
5. Codex
6. Vessel

Software and proof exist to protect the ritual, not replace it.

---

## 13. Non-goals

Sojourn 9 is not:

- a general journaling app
- an open social writing feed
- an NFT marketplace-first surface
- a speculation-first system
- a protocol for publishing raw writing onchain
- a broad AI writing assistant at the protocol layer

---

## 14. Implementation Boundary

Implementations may vary in rendering, backend architecture, storage approach, and reward settlement, provided they preserve:

- the 96-day / 12-region structure
- the 3,456-vessel limit
- one-wallet / one-vessel stewardship
- one seal per vessel per UTC day
- UTC day law
- no retroactive sealing
- the distinction between writer history and vessel history
- the privacy principle
- the rule that the chain records commitment, not content
- the centrality of today’s chamber

---

## 15. Final Law

Sojourn 9 succeeds when the system remains faithful to its simplicity.

There is one path.
There is one chamber today.
There is one vessel bound to the crossing.
There is one chance to seal the day.

Every decision must answer one question:

> Does this protect the rite and help the writer continue the crossing?
